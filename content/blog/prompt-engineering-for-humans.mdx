The best prompts aren't just code, but a conversation with a statistical ghost.
Treating a language model like a deterministic API misses the point — you are
steering a probability distribution, not calling a function.

## Think in intentions, not instructions

When you write a prompt, you are not issuing a command. You are nudging the model
toward a region of its latent space. The more context and intent you provide, the
more reliably it lands where you want.

- Describe the _role_ the model should play.
- Give _examples_ of the output you expect.
- Be explicit about _constraints_ and _format_.

## The conversation never ends

Iteration is the whole game. Your first prompt is a hypothesis; the output is data.
Refine, re-run, repeat — and soon you'll be having a genuine dialogue with the ghost.
