You can understand the math by building it yourself, without reaching for the
heavy libraries. Stripping away the abstractions is the fastest way to develop
real intuition.

## The forward pass

A neuron is just a weighted sum followed by a non-linearity. Stack a few of those
and you have a layer; stack a few layers and you have a network. That's it.

## Backpropagation is just the chain rule

The "magic" of training is nothing more than calculus applied carefully. Compute
the gradient of the loss with respect to each weight, take a small step in the
opposite direction, and repeat.

Once you've implemented this by hand, every framework afterward feels like a
convenience — never a mystery.
